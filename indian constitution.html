Constitution is the basic principles & laws of a Nation ,a State ,or a social group which determines the powers & duties of the Government and which gives certain rights to the people of it.

The Indian Constitution is also called as Bhartiya Samvidhan. India, also called as Bharat, is a Sovereign, Social, Secular Democratic, Republic Nation. The country is governed by the constitution, which was put together by the Constituent Assembly.

The Constitution of India was adopted on 26th November 1949. It came into affect on 26th January 1950.

We celebrate our Republic Day on 26th January of every year. To commemmorate the adoption of our Constitutiton we celebrate 26th November as NATIONAL CONSTITUTION DAY.

The Constitution of India is the largest constitution ever to be written by any country in the world. It was influenced by the Constitutions of other countries like the French Constitution, The Constitution of Japan, etc.

The President of the Constituent Assembly was Dr. Sacchhidhanandh Sinha. Dr. BR Ambeedkar was the chairman of it.

Dr. BR Ambedkar being the chaiman of the Constituent Assembly, played an important role in the drafting of the National Constitutiton.

It was the Congress Party, which demanded the Constituent Assembly for the first time. The people of India who were under the rule of the British Government, wanted their own constitution. However, the British Government accepted it in the year 1940.

Finally, on 26th January 1950, The CONSTITUTION OF INDIA was executed by the INDIAN GOVERNMENT.
